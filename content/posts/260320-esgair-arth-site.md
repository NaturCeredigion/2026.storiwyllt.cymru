After the first workshop for Ysgol Gynradd Aberaeron in the morning, I returned back up to a potential site just north of Aberaeron and inland from Aberarth, on the grounds of a farm called Esgair Arth. It is the old house of a famous local witch called Mari Berllan Peter, about whom our artist [Rebecca Wyn Kelly](https://www.rebeccawynkelly.com/) has done so much work already.

![Old ruined stone house, just mossy wall remaining](https://res.cloudinary.com/naturceredigion/image/upload/v1769119516/260122-witches-house-mari-berllan-peter.jpg)<br>*Mari Berllan Peter‘s old house by the Afon Arth*

The journey was made with the Stori Wyllt teacher Carys and the headteacher Anwen Lloyd-Hughes, to make sure the site is okay for their students. I had been to the site before with the estate manager Rowan Martin from Oxygen Conservation, who has been really supportive. But I have the worlds worst sense of direction, and I took them to the wrong corner of the field! 

If only I had looked at [Ceredigion Council‘s Rights of Way](https://ceredigion.gov.uk/resident/coast-countryside/public-rights-of-way/the-definitive-map-public-right-of-way-registers/rights-of-way-map/) map:

![Screenshot of a footpath map, showing footpath along track, and footpath across river](https://res.cloudinary.com/naturceredigion/image/upload/v1775132772/260402-council-prow-footpath-esgair-arth-mari-berllan-bitter.webp)<br>*The site of the house is by the dogleg in the footpath by the river*

After a last ditch search of the perimeter, I traced round red-faced back to the car and off we headed to Aberaeron but en-route we did see a magical Hare on the track. Mari Berllan Peter was rumoured to shape-shift into a Hare on occasion.
