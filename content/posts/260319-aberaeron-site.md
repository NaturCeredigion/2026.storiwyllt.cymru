Last year, we ran the Grassland ID workshops at Penmorfa with Ysgol Gynradd Aberaeron. 

{{< figure src="https://res.cloudinary.com/naturceredigion/image/upload/v1762376607/250516-aberaeron-penmorfa-children.jpg" alt="Students in bright red shirts on green sloping grassland with blue sea and sky" caption="Students from Ysgol Gynradd Aberaeron on Grassland ID workshop 2025" >}}

This year, we’re hoping for another magical spot, with a witch’s house, but this has yet to be confirmed. I had a chance meeting with my colleague, Tree Officer Norman Birch, and he explained that there is a public foot path in Aberaeron that runs on the south bank of the river Aeron, past the allotments an up to Jewsons.

Jewsons is coincidentally the site of the first [Tŵr Gwyllt](https://2026.storiwyllt.cymru/posts/260319-aberaeron-site/) (Wild Tower) installation, being designed and constructed by artist Rebecca Wyn Kelly, who is also the creative engaged with Stori Wyllt and Ysgol Gynradd Aberaeron.
